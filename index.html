bio

I am a postdoc with Ad Bax at the National Institutes of Health. I work on applying nuclear magnetic resonance spectroscopy to biophysical questions around protein folding.

I received my PhD from UIUC, where I worked with Chad Rienstra and focused on magic-angle spinning solid-state NMR. There I developed computational data analysis methods and studied \(\alpha\)-synuclein fibrils, the leading suspect in causing Parkinson's disease.
